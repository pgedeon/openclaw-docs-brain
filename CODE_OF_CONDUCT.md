# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in the Docs-Brain community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to fostering a welcoming and inclusive environment.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community and users
- Showing empathy towards other community members

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Offensive or harassing comments, in any medium
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory remarks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct. They are also responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and may take corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including GitHub repos, issue trackers, chat channels, and when representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project team at [opensource@openclaw.ai](mailto:opensource@openclaw.ai). All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The project team is obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter.
